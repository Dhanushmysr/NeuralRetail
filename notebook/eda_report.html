Quantity Distribution Analysis

Purpose: To understand the distribution of product quantities purchased in each transaction and identify skewness or unusual purchasing behavior.

In [3]:
plt.figure(figsize=(8,5))

sns.histplot(df["Quantity"], bins=40)

plt.title("Distribution of Quantity Sold")
plt.show()

Observation

Extreme right skew: Data heavily compresses at the lower bound. Massive volume spike: Over 800,000 transactions cluster near zero. Extended empty range: High-value outliers scale the axis to 80,000.

Business Insight

Retail dominant model: Revenue relies on high-frequency, low-quantity buyers. Skewed baseline metrics: Rare outliers distort standard average calculations. Dormant wholesale channel: Large bulk orders are isolated events.

Recommendation

Boost transaction size: Implement tiered volume discounts and bundle low-cost goods to lift average purchase values. Lock in recurring revenue: Launch a retail loyalty program and convert one-off bulk buyers into contractual B2B clients.

Transaction Revenue Box Plot (Outlier Analysis)

To detect outliers in transaction revenue, understand revenue variability, and identify unusually large purchases.

plt.title("Transaction Amount Boxplot")

plt.show()

Observation

The boxplot collapses tightly near zero, indicating a heavily compressed interquartile range with massive, isolated positive outliers reaching up to 168,000.

Business Insight

Standard retail operational metrics are completely warped by rare, hyper-large wholesale transactions that obscure the baseline realities of everyday customer behavior.

Recommendation

Segment transactional data by isolating everything above 15,000 into a distinct B2B reporting channel to allow for accurate retail trend analysis.

Key Business Insights

  • Volume-Driven Revenue: Low-cost, high-quantity item sales drive growth over premium unit pricing.
  • Severe Concentration Risk: Financial stability relies entirely on the UK market and two VIP clients.
  • Extreme Holiday Seasonality: Sales peak massively in November and drop sharply during Q1.
  • Midday Weekday Purchasing: Transaction traffic concentrates on weekdays, peaking sharply at midday.
  • Outlier-Warped Metrics: Rare, massive wholesale orders heavily distort standard retail averages.

Conclusion

The business operates a highly successful, high-volume transactional model anchored by low-cost novelty goods, a dominant UK presence, and strong Q4 seasonal demand. However, this success is heavily reliant on a small group of high-volume wholesale outliers and just two key VIP clients, introducing significant concentration and operational risks. Moving forward, the company can sustain its growth by diversifying into international markets, formalizing B2B contracts, and shifting its operational scheduling to match its clear midday and weekday customer traffic peaks.
